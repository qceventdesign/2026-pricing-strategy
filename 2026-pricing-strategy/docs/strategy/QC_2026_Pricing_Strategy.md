# QC 2026 Pricing Strategy

**Internal Reference Guide**

This document outlines Quill Creative's pricing framework for 2026, including category-based markups, operational cost allocation, program profitability evaluation, and decision rules for program management fees.

**Effective January 1, 2026**

*Prepared for internal team use only.*

## 1. Category-Based Markup Schedule

Markup is applied by vendor category, not as a flat rate. The logic: mark up higher where clients have no benchmark to compare, and hold the floor where they do. Every category has a specific floor based on the coordination, expertise, and risk QC absorbs.

| Vendor Category | Markup | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Catering & F&B | 54% | Clients can compare per-head pricing. Hold the floor. |
| Venues & Room Rentals | 60% | Competitive category; sourcing and negotiation adds value. Push to 60% when managing full venue relationship. |
| AV & Production | 65% | Technical coordination, load-in management, vendor liaison. Harder for clients to price-shop. |
| Décor & Design | 85% | Fully custom creative work. No apples-to-apples comparison exists. This is design expertise — price it accordingly. |
| Entertainment | 75% | Talent sourcing, contract negotiation, rider management, logistics. Clients rarely know what acts cost. |
| Activities & Experiences | 75% | Curation, coordination, permitting, logistics. Selling the idea and the execution, not just a vendor. |
| Transportation | 75% | Route planning, driver coordination, timing, backup plans. High-liability, high-coordination. |
| Staffing & Labor | 90% | Recruitment, briefing, scheduling, onsite management. QC owns the outcome, not just the hourly rate. |
| Purchased / Sourced Items | 200% | Procurement, handling, inventory, setup, disposal. Small dollar items that take disproportionate time (3× cost). |
| Delivery & Logistics | 85% | Timing risk, damage liability, coordination. Clients don't know what delivery costs. |
| Tours & Guided Experiences | 65% | Guide sourcing, route design, F&B coordination. Fixed costs (guides, coordinators) scale across group sizes. |

**Absolute Floor: 54% markup on any line item. No exceptions without owner approval.**

*Blended Effect: On a typical multi-vendor program, this schedule produces a blended markup of 65–75% depending on category mix — meaningfully above the 54% floor and sufficient to cover real operational costs.*

## 2. Hours-Based OpEx Allocation

Instead of applying a flat percentage to every program, estimate the actual team hours the program will consume. This gives a true cost-to-serve and prevents small programs from quietly draining profitability.

**Internal Hourly Rate: $90/hr**

This accounts for salary, benefits, overhead, and non-billable time. Midpoint of the $75–$100 range.

| Tier | Description | Hours | OpEx Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Light | Single-vendor, simple logistics. Restaurant buyout, one activity, ticket sourcing. | 8–20 hrs | $720–$1,800 |
| Standard | Multi-vendor, single-day event. Venue + catering + décor + entertainment + transport. | 25–50 hrs | $2,250–$4,500 |
| Complex | Multi-day, multi-vendor, onsite staffing. Full incentive program, conference support, multi-city. | 50–100 hrs | $4,500–$9,000 |
| Enterprise | Large-scale, multi-day with design builds, production, multiple venues, 50+ guests. | 100–160+ hrs | $9,000–$14,400+ |

**How to use:** When estimating a program, categorize it by tier, estimate hours, and multiply by $90. That number — not a blanket percentage — is what the program needs to cover beyond vendor costs and commissions.

## 3. Commission Structure

Commission scenarios affect margin thresholds. QC must price differently depending on the commission load on a given program.

| Scenario | Commission | CC Fee | Net to QC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct Client (no commission) | 0% | 3.5% | 96.5% |
| GDP Standard (no client commission) | 6.5% | 3.5% | 90.0% |
| GDP + Client Commission | 21.5% | 3.5% | 75.0% |

The 21.5% commission scenario (GDP 6.5% + client agent 15%) represents the most aggressive margin compression. Programs in this scenario require higher markups or visible management fees to remain profitable.

## 4. Program P&L Evaluation Framework

Every estimate should be evaluated against this framework before it goes to the client. The Master Pricing Template automates this calculation.

```
CLIENT INVOICE TOTAL
− Client Commission (if any)
− GDP Fee (6.5%)
− Total Vendor Costs
= QC GROSS REVENUE → QC Margin %
− Estimated OpEx (hours × $90)
= TRUE NET PROFIT → True Net Margin %
```

### Target Thresholds

| Metric | Floor | Target | Stretch |
|---|---|---|---|
| QC Margin % (no commission) | 35% | 42–45% | 50%+ |
| QC Margin % (6.5% GDP only) | 30% | 38–42% | 45%+ |
| QC Margin % (21.5% commission) | 22% | 28–32% | 35%+ |
| True Net Margin % | 5% | 10–15% | 18%+ |

## 5. Margin Health Indicators

### QC Gross Margin

| Indicator | Threshold | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ✓ STRONG | ≥ 45% | Healthy margin. Program is well-priced. |
| → ON TARGET | ≥ 35% | Acceptable. Monitor for scope creep. |
| ⚠ REVIEW | ≥ 25% | Thin margin. May not cover OpEx. Adjust pricing or add management fee. |
| ✗ BELOW FLOOR | < 25% | Below minimum. Do not proceed without owner approval and pricing adjustment. |

### True Net Margin

| Indicator | Threshold | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ✓ STRONG | ≥ 15% | Profitable after all costs including team time. |
| → ON TARGET | ≥ 7% | Acceptable net return. Keep hours in check. |
| ⚠ THIN | ≥ 0% | Breaking even on team time. Only justified for strategic relationship value. |
| ✗ LOSING MONEY | < 0% | Program costs more in team time than it generates. Walk away or restructure. |

## 6. Decision Rules

**When to Add a Visible Program Management Fee**
- Programs over $40K where the scope includes onsite staffing, multi-day coordination, or design work.
- Fee range: $2,500–$10,000 depending on complexity.
- Fortune 500 clients expect this line item. It signals professionalism.

**When to Absorb Into Markup Instead**
- Programs under $40K or returning clients where a new line item would create friction.
- Bake coordination time into higher markups on décor, staffing, and logistics categories.

**When to Walk Away or Set Minimum Fees**
- If True Net Profit is negative after hours-based OpEx and pricing cannot be adjusted.
- Add a minimum program fee of $1,500–$2,500 or decline the work.
- Small programs with the same coordination overhead as large ones are the highest-risk category.

**When to Flex Below the Markup Floor**
- Strategic relationship plays where the client represents recurring high-value work.
- Accept thin margins on an individual program only if the annual relationship math works.
- **Document the decision. Do not let it become the default.**
